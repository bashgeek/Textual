# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
People downloading Textwerk, a modern fork of the Textual IRC client for macOS. A mix of two groups: longtime Textual/IRC users (often nostalgic Mac nerds who've used IRC for years) and newer users discovering IRC through this app. Primarily viewed on desktop (it's a desktop Mac app being marketed), but mobile traffic happens too and should work well, not just "not be broken."

### Brand Personality
Polished, modern, stable, fast. The user likes Apple's design sensibility as a reference point but does not want the site to closely mimic Apple's own marketing pages — it should have its own personality while still feeling "modern, Mac-related, nice." Currently the site leans very close to an Apple.com pastiche (SF system font stack, pill buttons, centered hero, violet accent) — the direction is to keep the polish/quality bar Apple sets, but differentiate the visual identity.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Light theme confirmed as the right call (desktop-first product page, browsed in normal daylight/work contexts).
- Should read as trustworthy and current, not generic-startup or README-as-a-website.
- Own visual identity: distinct typography and color point of view rather than borrowing Apple's exact system-font-and-pill-button formula.
- No stated hard anti-references beyond "not a close Apple clone."

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize desktop layout quality first (this is a desktop app's marketing page), but never let mobile be an afterthought — adapt, don't just shrink or hide nav items.
2. Match Apple's bar for polish and restraint, without borrowing Apple's literal visual formula (SF Pro, pill buttons, violet-on-white).
3. Give the brand its own typographic and color identity distinct from generic AI/SaaS marketing tropes.
4. Every visual choice should reinforce "stable, fast, modern" — avoid anything that reads as dated (skeuomorphic IRC clichés) or trend-chasing (AI-slop gradients/glassmorphism).
